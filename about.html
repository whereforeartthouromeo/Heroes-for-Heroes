Who are we?

We are a group of students from Crescenta Valley High School in La Crescenta, California.


"At eleven years old, I was in the Children's Hospital Los Angeles for a hernia surgery. The surgery was delayed for many hours, and I began to feel bored, anxious, and hungry, which added on to my preexisting fear of the surgery. My family was there, so I tried to hide my angst, but they noticed and asked the doctors for some form of entertainment. The doctors wheeled in a cart shortly after, which had an Xbox 360 and a TV. In no time, my stress was gone, and I began to feel positive about the surgery. The surgery was a success, and looking back on it the entertainment cart, something that is so simple, completely changed my perspective on my visit. Years later, I read an article on actor Chris Pratt and athlete Russell Wilson visiting sick children in a Seattle Hospital. This got me thinking about my surgery, and how something as simple as an entertainment cart or a visit from someone's hero can make a poor situation positive. I wanted to run a similar operation, but with visitors more fitting for the child and at a higher availability. And thus, with help from my friend Andrew Boyle, the nonprofit organization Hospital Heroes was created."

- Keegan Schmit
Founder and President



What is our goal?

We aim to run a successful organization that improves a hospital's atmosphere for young patients.


Who are our members?

Our members include President and Head of Technology Keegan Schmit, Vice president Andrew Boyle, Treasurer Abel Lai, Publicist Solomon Kim, and Head of Communication and Outreach Tyler Carlson.


When and where were we founded?

Hospital Heroes was founded in La Crescenta, California in 2016.


How can you make a difference?

You can make a difference by signing up on our "Volunteer" page, or by donating money on our "Donate" page. Any donation of any size is appreciated.


Who do we help, and how can you see about scheduling a visit for your child?

We help exclusively youth patients, and mainly long-term or terminal ones, but there are exceptions. You may visit our "Refer a child" page to request a visit for your child.


How can you get a hold of us?

You can find all our contact information on the "Contact" page.


Just hospital visits?

No, we also organize blood drives in the La Crescenta area and fundraise for "fun carts"
